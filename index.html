Experience Narrative

Rivian (VW Tech)

Sept 2025 – Present

Prototype Analyst, Program Management

At Rivian (VW Tech), my primary mission is ensuring prototype electric vehicles get the exact parts they need to be built on time, without delays. To do this, I architected live digital dashboards and Python-based automated reporting tools that sit between human suppliers and complex manufacturing data. By automating these workflows, I eliminated manual data entry, successfully resolved critical supply shortages for over 2,200 parts, and secured the on-time delivery of major prototype builds (LabCars) for 5 international OEMs. I primarily utilized Python, Google App Script, advanced supply chain analytics, and custom KPI dashboards to execute this.

Case New Holland

May 2025 – June 2025

Senior Lead Designer, Interior Systems

For Case New Holland, I led the end-to-end redesign of the Magnum tractor's interior cabin. The goal was to aggressively improve the layout so the operator could comfortably perform their daily tasks with better ergonomics and visibility. During this process, I introduced agile 3D printing methodologies over traditional plastic tooling. My financial assessments proved this would drastically reduce material costs, shifting the brand's production strategy and securing a $3M+ budget for the next-generation iteration. I utilized SolidWorks, 3D printing farm operations, and deep financial modeling.

Ford

Mar 2024 – Dec 2024

Design Engineer, Interior Systems

Working on next-generation electric vehicles at Ford, my focus was on structural optimization—specifically ensuring the internal seat frames were as efficient, robust, and cost-effective as possible. I applied rigorous Design for Assembly (DFMA) principles directly within complex 3D CAD platforms, ultimately cutting mass-production costs by $270,000 annually. Additionally, I overhauled the company's physical 3D-printing testing processes to drive a lab-wide scrap reduction initiative, aggressively pushing forward our corporate sustainability goals. I utilized CATIA 3DX, DFMA methodologies, and physical lab prototyping.

Navistar & Webasto

Nov 2019 – Mar 2024

Engineering Journey across Design & DFMEA

My engineering journey began with intense cooperative work leading aerodynamic drafting and production design for commercial and automotive leaders. At Webasto, I ran complex CAD models to perfect latch designs for automotive roof systems. Growing into a role at Navistar, I transitioned to applying rigid Six Sigma methodologies (DFSS) to validate physical designs that were ready for mass production. This combination of hands-on supplier engagement and deep 3D modeling expertise built the very foundation of my data-driven engineering approach. I utilized CATIA, Six Sigma (DFSS), Aerodynamic Modeling, and Tier-1 Supplier Negotiations.
